FAQ

Launch FAQ for benchmark readers

Short answers for people arriving from search, GitHub, or a shared benchmark link who need to understand what this page says before they trust the ranking.

Question 1

What does this benchmark actually measure?

It compares first visible partial timing, partial cadence, final transcript latency, REST latency, runtime profile, and sample coverage from checked-in artifacts so voice AI teams can judge turn-taking readiness instead of relying on anecdotal latency claims.

Question 2

Why are some tracked lanes missing from the comparison table?

Only lanes with committed publishable JSON artifacts appear in the public ranking. Registry-only, blocked, or unpublished lanes stay documented in the track registry without polluting the homepage comparison flow.

Question 3

Does this page compare recognition accuracy too?

Yes, but only as clearly labeled reference WER sourced from upstream evaluations for the underlying model. Those figures are not official rtc-asr measurements and may vary slightly across hardware, runtime, quantization, decoding, and setup.

Question 4

How should a WebRTC or voice AI team use this before launch?

Use the homepage to shortlist realistic ASR lanes, check the benchmark notes for reproduction details, and then validate the finalists against your own codec, interruption, and domain-specific traffic before changing production defaults.
